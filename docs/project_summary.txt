1. SCIENTIFIC CONTEXT & ETHICAL PROBLEM STATEMENT
Public genomic repositories scale rapidly but are highly prone to legacy manual 
metadata misclassification. A systemic vulnerability in clinical data interpretation 
is the premature classification of strains based purely on broad phenotypic profiles, 
such as labeling a specimen "MRSA" without verifying its underlying genetic architecture. 

When uncharacterized isolates are forced through rigid, reference-guided alignment 
scaffolds, traditional variant-calling tools encounter a structural blind spot: they 
cannot map highly divergent sequences or large horizontal gene transfer (HGT) events 
that do not exist in the susceptible baseline template. This project establishes a 
reproducible, coordinate-independent validation framework designed to systematically 
deconvolute reference-mapping bias, protect dataset integrity, and uncover true 
mechanistic alternatives behind deceptive clinical phenotypes.

- Adapter & Quality Trimming: Trimmomatic stringency filtering (SLIDINGWINDOW:4:20).

- Post-Processing Optimization: Samtools coordinate-sorting and indexing.
- Duplicate Remediation: Picard MarkDuplicates isolation of artificial PCR clones.
- Genotypic Invariant Mapping: GATK HaplotypeCaller de novo variant discovery.
- Strict Quality Filtration: GATK VariantFiltration (Thresholds: QD < 2.0, FS > 60.0).
